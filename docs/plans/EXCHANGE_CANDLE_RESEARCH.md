OHLC construction — last-trade, not average\nReal exchanges and every mainstream charting platform build a candle by **time-bucketing raw trades**: **Open = first trade in the interval, Close = the last trade, High/Low = max/min trade prices** during the window. Close is the *final executed price*, never an average of the interval ([QuestDB](https://questdb.com/glossary/ohlc-candlestick/); [Phemex](https://phemex.com/academy/what-is-ohlc-open-high-low-close-chart); [Cube](https://www.cube.exchange/what-is/ohlc-candlesticks)). Averages appear only in **derived overlays**, not in the raw candle: **VWAP** = Cumulative(Volume × TypicalPrice) / Cumulative(Volume), where **Typical Price = (H+L+C)/3** ([StockCharts VWAP](https://chartschool.stockcharts.com/table-of-contents/technical-indicators-and-overlays/technical-overlays/volume-weighted-average-price-vwap)). \"Average price bars\" / typical-price series exist but are explicitly *secondary indicators layered on top of* raw OHLC.\n\n## 2. The owner's hypothesis — mostly false as a *display convention*\n\"Exchanges show the interval **average** instead of the last tick to smooth it out\" is **not** how standard price charts work. The canonical candle Close is the last trade, full stop. However, the hypothesis is a *good description of derived tools*: VWAP shows a volume-weighted average and is smoother, and **Heikin-Ashi** literally replaces Close with the bar's 4-price average. So the intuition (\"averaging smooths\") is correct — it's just achieved via **overlays/alternative candle types**, not by redefining the exchange's official Close ([StockCharts VWAP](https://chartschool.stockcharts.com/table-of-contents/technical-indicators-and-overlays/technical-overlays/volume-weighted-average-price-vwap); [Investopedia/HDFC on Heikin-Ashi](https://hdfcsky.com/sky-learn/trading-strategies/heikin-ashi-candlestick-pattern)).\n\n## 3. Cons: hides true open/close, lags reversals (~2-period smoothing), unusable for exact fills. Other smoothing types: **Renko** (fixed-size bricks, time-independent, filters sub-brick noise), **Line Break**, **Hollow candles** — all noise filters, all derived views ([StockCharts Renko](https://chartschool.stockcharts.com/table-of-contents/chart-analysis/chart-types/renko-charts)).\n\n## 4. Continuity — close[t] vs open[t+1]\nWithin a continuous session **consecutive real candles usually connect** (open[t+1] ≈ close[t]) because the next trade is near the last. A **gap is real** only when *no trading spans the boundary* — overnight/weekend/halt — and news repriced the asset, so the market \"jumps rather than walking there tick by tick\" ([Mind-the-Gap/Octopu](https://www.tradingview.com/script/odT1EsXG-); [DayTradingToolkit](https://daytradingtoolkit.com/beginners-guide/understanding-stock-gaps-explained)). **Line charts** plot only Close, so they are *always* visually continuous (each point connects to the next); **candlesticks** expose the open-vs-prior-close gap, which is why they look \"jumpier.\" For **no-trade intervals**, two legitimate conventions exist: **omit the bar** (clean data, leaves a gap) or **carry forward last close** → a flat doji (O=H=L=C=prevClose), which looks continuous ([Massive](https://massive.com/knowledge-base/article/how-does-massive-create-the-open-high-low-close-volume-aggregate-bars); [Domo](https://www.domo.com/learn/charts/ohlc-chart)).\n\n## 5. Missing bars / gaps — standard handling\nConsensus from data vendors: **never interpolate** — fabricated bars imply trades that never happened (\"phantom price action\"). Real exchanges **either skip the bar or carry the last close forward**; forward-fill (flat doji) is the display-friendly choice, blank/omit is the data-purity choice ([CoinAPI OHLCV](https://www.coinapi.io/blog/ohlcv-data-explained-real-time-updates-websocket-behavior-and-trading-applications); [Massive](https://massive.com/knowledge-base/article/how-does-massive-create-the-open-high-low-close-volume-aggregate-bars)).\n\n## 6.
